Self-Defense Against Sharks

Learn how to protect yourself if you encounter a shark while swimming or surfing.

Stay Calm

The most important thing when encountering a shark is to stay calm. Panicking can lead to mistakes. Maintain eye contact and try to keep yourself stable in the water.

Defend Yourself

If a shark attacks, defend yourself by targeting its most sensitive areas: the eyes, gills, or snout. Use any available objects, such as a spear or surfboard, to push it away.

Get Out of the Water

If you can, slowly and steadily move towards the shore. Avoid splashing or making erratic movements. Getting out of the water safely is key in avoiding further danger.
