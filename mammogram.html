Mammogram Guidelines and Importance

  • When to Start: Women should begin regular mammograms at age 40. If you have a family history of breast cancer, you may need to start earlier.
  • Frequency: Mammograms should be done every year or as advised by your healthcare provider.
  • What to Expect: A mammogram involves an X-ray of the breasts. It's important to inform the technician if you're pregnant or might be pregnant.
  • Why It's Important: Mammograms are crucial for detecting breast cancer early, which can lead to better outcomes and more treatment options.
  • After the Mammogram: Results will be sent to your doctor. If there are concerns, your doctor may recommend additional tests, such as an ultrasound or biopsy.
